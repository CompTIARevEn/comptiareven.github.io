CySA+ Sales Enablement — Proficiency Worksheet
CySA+ Sales Enablement — Proficiency Worksheet ~15–20 min  |  4 Parts
1
Product Knowledge
5 short-answer prompts  ·  ~3 minutes
1.1 Explain the difference between CertMaster Perform and CertMaster Learn. When would you recommend each, and for which customer type?
1.2 Name the four CySA+ CS0-004 exam domains and their weights. Which is the largest, and what's new in that domain in V4?
1.3 Name three things that are new or significantly expanded in CySA+ V4 that were not in V3. Why does each matter to a customer?
1.4 Describe the Learning Progression Model and its three lab stages. Why is this a differentiator in a Government or Enterprise sales conversation?
1.5 A Government customer asks why CySA+ specifically meets DoD 8140 requirements. What three facts do you lead with?
2
Customer Application
3 scenario prompts  ·  ~5 minutes
2.1 Government — Write your recommended approach, leading with the most relevant differentiator for this customer.
Customer: Brigadier General Thomas Reyes, Director of Cyber Workforce, U.S. Space Force — responsible for credentialing 45 cybersecurity personnel under DoD 8140 by Q2 2027. 30 analysts have Security+ but no intermediate credential. Budget: $120K. Comparing CySA+ against GIAC GCIA.
2.2 Enterprise — How do you respond?
Customer: Marcus Chen, CISO, CloudFirst Technology — cloud-native SaaS company using AWS and GCP. 8-person security team. Marcus asks: "Does CySA+ cover cloud-specific tools and techniques, or is it primarily for on-premises environments?"
2.3 Enterprise Academic Higher Ed — How do you respond?
Customer: Dr. James Okafor, Dean of Engineering and Technology, Northshore Community College — 150 cybersecurity students in a two-year AAS program. 70% enter SOC analyst or IT security roles. Dr. Okafor asks: "How do I justify adding CySA+ as a graduation requirement to my faculty and board — and what's the ROI argument for students paying the exam fee?"
3
Demo & Story Fluency
Sequencing, matching & story purpose  ·  ~4 minutes
3.1 Number these demo steps 1 (first) through 5 (last) in the correct order for a 10-minute customer demo.
Show a Simulation Lab — guided, step-by-step tool practice
Show a VM Lab — contrast with simulation lab (live environment, no instructions)
Open with a brief overview statement connecting the product to the customer's specific problem
Show an Activity (performance-based task mirroring the exam)
Close with a specific next step connected to the customer's problem
3.2 Match each customer to the strongest narrative thread.
A defense contractor credentialing 30 analysts under DoD 8140 by Q4
A community college adding CySA+ as a program exit requirement for cybersecurity graduates
A financial firm whose analysts failed the exam using self-study materials
A cloud-native company using AWS and GCP skeptical of "generic" certifications
3.3 Write a one-sentence Story Purpose for this customer.
Customer: Sofia Reyes, Director of Security Operations, Apex Healthcare Systems — 18-person security team. Two analysts recently left for roles requiring CySA+. Sofia wants to credential her remaining team to prevent further attrition and meet emerging compliance requirements from her health system's cyber insurance provider.
4
Applied Judgment
2 complex scenarios  ·  ~5 minutes
4.1 Three-way competitive evaluation (Government) — Write your recommendation and reasoning. Which thread do you lead with, which competitor do you prioritize countering, and how do you close?
Situation: A large federal agency is comparing CySA+ (CertMaster Perform) against Microsoft SC-200 and GIAC GCIA. Requirements: (1) DoD 8140 approved credential, (2) hands-on skills validation beyond multiple choice, (3) total cost under $3,000 per analyst. The procurement officer is leaning toward GIAC GCIA because "SANS has a strong reputation in government."
4.2 Academic skeptic (Enterprise Academic Higher Ed) — How do you respond? Be specific about what CySA+ provides and how it complements project-based learning — not replaces it.
Situation: A university department chair says: "Our industry advisory board says employers care more about hands-on project experience than certifications. Why should we add CySA+ when our students already build projects in our capstone courses?"
