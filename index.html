Part 2 Customer Application

Complete 2 of the 4 scenarios below — your own vertical plus one other. Reviewing all four is encouraged for situational awareness.

Scenario 2.1 — Enterprise
Chen Wei, Director of Cloud Infrastructure — Apex Logistics Corp.
Apex runs a hybrid AWS + on-prem environment with 45 infrastructure engineers. Chen's team is asked to automate provisioning and deployments by Q4 — but only 12 engineers have any IaC or scripting experience. Training budget is available.
What is your recommended approach — which product(s), which differentiator to lead with, and how you'd open the conversation?
Scenario 2.2 — Government
Sandra Reeves, IT Training Coordinator — Defense Health Agency
Sandra manages workforce development for 120 IT staff across multiple DHA sites. She's been asked to identify certifications that could support DoD 8140 compliance planning. Budget is available but must be obligated before September 30.
How do you respond to Sandra's 8140 question — and what's your next step to keep this opportunity moving before her budget deadline?
Scenario 2.3 — Academic (Higher Ed)
Professor Dana Kowalski, IT Program Coordinator — Ridgemont Technical College
Dana is building a cloud administration certificate program for working adults (ages 25–45, already employed in IT). She wants a cert employers recognize that students can complete in one semester alongside full-time work.
How do you position AutoOps+ for Dana — and which product or bundle would you recommend for her student profile?
Scenario 2.4 — Partner
Derek Huang, Director of Curriculum — TechPath Training Solutions
Derek delivers instructor-led training to enterprise and government clients across multiple cloud environments. He's evaluating AutoOps+ for his catalog but is concerned about how to build ILT curriculum without CertMaster Learn.
How do you address Derek's concern about CertMaster Learn — and what does an AutoOps+ ILT solution look like for his business?

Part 4 Applied Judgment

Complete one prompt per vertical — choose the prompt that matches your primary channel, but review all four for situational and competitive awareness.

Scenario 4.1 — Two Buyers, One Meeting
Enterprise Version
You're meeting with a VP of IT Operations (cares about day-to-day automation skills) and a CISO (cares about security automation and compliance). You have 20 minutes and two stakeholders with different definitions of value. How do you structure your AutoOps+ story to serve both without losing either?
Government Version
You're meeting with an IT Training Coordinator (cares about workforce credentialing) and a Contracting Officer (cares about procurement compliance and approved vendor lists). Same meeting, different priorities. How do you navigate both?
Academic Version
You're presenting to a Department Chair (cares about curriculum fit and employer recognition) and a Dean of Workforce Development (cares about grant compliance and enrollment impact). How do you frame AutoOps+ for both in one conversation?
Partner Version
You're presenting to a Partner's Director of Curriculum (cares about content quality and delivery flexibility) and their Sales Director (cares about margin and client demand). How do you make the case for adding AutoOps+ to their catalog?
Scenario 4.2 — Objection Under Pressure
Enterprise Version
You're two weeks from closing a 40-seat AutoOps+ deal. The training manager calls to say their CFO has asked why they shouldn't send the team to a 3-day intensive workshop instead — it's cheaper and faster. The deal is at risk. How do you respond?
Government Version
You're close to closing a government training contract. The procurement officer now says a competing vendor has claimed their cert already has DoD 8140 approval. How do you respond accurately without overpromising on AutoOps+ accreditation status?
Academic Version
A curriculum committee has approved AutoOps+ in principle, but a faculty member now argues that students should pursue a vendor-specific cert because "employers recognize AWS more than CompTIA." How do you address this objection through the department chair?
Partner Version
A partner you've been working with for months calls to say one of their major clients is pushing back on AutoOps+ because they've heard the cert is "too new" and unproven. The partner is considering dropping the course from their catalog. How do you keep the relationship and the deal?
